FAQs About Amazon PPC

What is Amazon PPC? +
Amazon PPC (Pay-Per-Click) is a form of paid advertising on Amazon where advertisers pay a fee each time their ad is clicked. It's a powerful tool to drive traffic to product listings and increase sales.
How does Amazon PPC work? +
Amazon PPC allows sellers to bid on keywords related to their products. When a shopper searches for these keywords, the highest bidders' ads appear in the search results or product pages. Advertisers only pay when someone clicks on their ad.
What are the types of Amazon PPC ads? +
Amazon PPC offers three types of ads: Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display. Sponsored Products promote individual listings, Sponsored Brands highlight your brand and a selection of products, and Sponsored Display targets shoppers based on their behavior.
How can I optimize my Amazon PPC campaigns? +
To optimize Amazon PPC campaigns, focus on keyword research, bid management, and monitoring performance. Regularly review your campaigns, adjust bids for underperforming keywords, and test new keywords to improve results.
What is ACoS in Amazon PPC? +
ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sales) is a key metric in Amazon PPC that shows the ratio of ad spend to sales revenue generated. A lower ACoS indicates a more profitable campaign. To calculate ACoS, divide ad spend by revenue.
